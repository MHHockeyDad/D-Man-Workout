⚠ Youth Athlete Safety Guidelines
  • All strength work uses light loads — technique and range of motion always come first. Form over weight, every time.
  • Never skip warm-up or cool-down. Growing bodies need proper prep and recovery.
  • Rest days are training days. Sleep 9–10 hours. Hydrate continuously.
  • If anything hurts (not just "burns") — STOP. Pain is a signal, not a challenge.
  • Adjust weights based on feel: if form breaks down, drop the weight immediately.
  • Always have an adult present for free weight exercises.
🛡 How This Program Differs from the Forward Program
  • Backward skating starts Week 1 — it's a primary focus from day one, not an add-on. Every blade session has structured backward skating built in.
  • Pivot transitions are a weekly staple — forward-to-backward and backward-to-forward pivots are practiced on blades every single week, increasing in complexity and speed.
  • Gap control sprints replace forward rush patterns — D-men retreat, read, then close. Drill patterns reflect that: back-up → read → close out.
  • Lateral mirror shuffles replace wing routes — shadowing an attacker side-to-side while maintaining positioning is trained directly.
  • Posterior chain + rotational core emphasis — slightly more volume on hip hinge, glutes, and anti-rotation core for board battles and defensive leverage.
  • Single-leg jump progression is identical — same 12-week build as the forward program. Single-leg power is just as critical for D-men push-off and gap closing.

Program StructureTHREE-PHASE PROGRESSION
Phase I — Weeks 1–4
Foundation & D-Zone Mechanics
WEEKS 1 – 4
Build movement vocabulary with a defensive lens. A/B/C-skips, backward skating fundamentals, basic pivot mechanics, hip flexor activation, body weight and light dumbbell strength. Learn to accelerate backwards and transition smoothly.
Phase II — Weeks 5–8
Gap Control & Power
WEEKS 5 – 8
Increase backward skating speed, add pivot complexity, introduce gap-closing sprint patterns, light barbell work, lateral mirror drills, and single-leg jump progressions. First-step reads and reactive closing speed.
Phase III — Weeks 9–12
Competition Prep & Peak
WEEKS 9 – 12
Game-speed defensive patterns, full retreat-read-close sequences, board battle conditioning, max single-leg power, and full-speed pivot transitions. Peak everything built in Phases I and II.
Equipment Used in This Program

Cones · Agility Ladder · Dumbbells (5 lb, 8 lb, 10 lb max) · Free Weights / Barbell (bodyweight to 15 lb bar only) · Roller Blades · Bike

WK 01
D-Zone Movement Foundation
Phase I · Foundation
+

Low intensity. Learn forward AND backward movement patterns simultaneously. Everything is technique-first.

Monday — Skip Mechanics + Backward Intro
  • Warm-up: 5 min easy jog + dynamic leg swings, hip circles, ankle rolls
  • A-Skips: 3 × 22 yds forward
  • B-Skips: 3 × 22 yds forward
  • Backward Run (intro): 3 × 22 yds — slow, high knees, push off ball of foot behind you
  • Carioca: 3 × 22 yds each direction
  • Single-Leg Hop (intro): 3 × 4 hops each leg — land on same foot, pause 1s, balance focus
  • Cool-down: Hip flexor 60s each side, quad, hamstring stretch
Tuesday — Ladder + Core
  • Warm-up: 5 min easy bike or jog
  • Ladder — 1-in, 1-out: 4 × down/back
  • Ladder — 2-in, 2-out: 4 × down/back
  • Ladder — Icky Shuffle: 3 × (learn slowly)
  • Plank: 3 × 20s
  • Dead Bug: 3 × 8 each side
  • Glute Bridge: 3 × 12
  • Pallof Press (anti-rotation — use resistance or towel): 3 × 10 each side
  • Cool-down: Child's pose, pigeon 45s each side
Wednesday — Backward Skating Foundation
  • Blades: 25 min total
  • 8 min forward easy warm-up cruise
  • Backward skating 22 yds × 8 — push C-cuts, stay low, chin up
  • Forward pivot to backward (stationary): 10 × each direction — plant foot, swing hips, drop into backward stride
  • 5 min forward cool-down + hip flexor stretch
Thursday — Hip Flexor + Body Weight Strength
  • Warm-up: Leg swings forward/back + side-to-side × 15 each
  • Hip Flexor March (standing): 3 × 12 each leg
  • Reverse Lunge: 3 × 8 each leg — bodyweight only
  • Lateral Lunge: 3 × 8 each side
  • Wall Sit: 3 × 20s
  • Push-ups: 3 × 8
  • Superman Hold: 3 × 10s — builds posterior chain for puck battles
  • Cool-down: Kneeling lunge 60s, IT band stretch
Friday — Gap Control Cones + Endurance
  • Warm-up: 5 min A-skip + backward run review
  • Backward-to-Forward Sprint (gap close): 5 × — back-pedal 11 yds, stop, sprint forward 11 yds
  • T-Drill: 4 × (learn the pattern)
  • Box Drill (4 cones): 4 × each direction
  • Single-Leg Box Step-Up (8" step): 3 × 8 each leg
  • Endurance Bike: 15 min steady (conversational pace)
  • Cool-down: Full stretch 5 min
Sat / Sun — Full Rest
  • Sleep 9–10 hours
  • Stay hydrated — 60+ oz water/day
  • Light walk or casual play only

Reference GuideDRILL LIBRARY
Skip Mechanics
A-Skip
March with exaggerated knee drive, opposite arm forward, drive the ball of foot straight down. Builds sprint mechanics and hip flexor activation — same value for D-men as forwards.
Skip Mechanics
B-Skip
Start with an A-skip then extend the raised leg fully forward before driving the foot down and back. Trains the full sprint leg cycle — hamstring loading and pawing action.
Skip Mechanics
C-Skip (Lateral)
A lateral version of the A-skip. Drive the inside knee up and across while moving sideways. Builds hip abductor and adductor strength — critical for the sideways push of a defensive crossover.
D-Man Specific
Backward C-Cut Run
Running backward by pushing off alternating feet in a C-shape, staying low (knees bent ~90°), chin up, eyes forward. The fundamental backward skating movement pattern. Push hard through the heel of each C-cut for power.
D-Man Specific
Forward-to-Backward Pivot
While moving forward, plant one foot, swing the hips around, and drop into a backward stride. The pivot foot stays on the ground as the anchor. Practice both left-foot pivot and right-foot pivot — defensemen must be equally fluid both directions.
D-Man Specific
Retreat-Read-Close
Skate backward to a set cone (simulating gap control), pivot at speed, then explode forward to close out. The three-part sequence every D-man uses on zone entries. Timed weekly to track improvement.
D-Man Specific
Lateral Mirror Shuffle
Shuffle laterally between two cones, staying low and square, no crossover steps. Simulates mirroring an attacker's lateral movement. In later weeks, add a sprint out at the end to simulate a close-out.
Agility
T-Drill
Sprint 10 yards, shuffle left 5 yards, shuffle right 10 yards, shuffle back left 5 yards, backpedal to start. One of the best tests of multi-directional speed — used for all three assessments.
Agility
5-10-5 (Pro Agility)
From center, sprint 5 yards right, touch line, sprint 10 yards left, touch line, sprint 5 yards back. Tests change of direction and deceleration — tracked at Weeks 4, 8, and 12.
Hip Flexor
Kneeling Hip Flexor Stretch
Drop to one knee, drive hips forward keeping core braced. Add overhead reach on the kneeling side for a full psoas stretch. Hold 60–90 seconds each side. Critical for backward skating hip extension range.
Core / Stability
Pallof Press
Using a resistance band or towel anchored at chest height beside you, hold the band at your chest and press it straight out in front — resist the rotation. The anti-rotation demand directly trains the core stability needed for board battles and puck protection.
Strength
Goblet Squat
Hold one dumbbell at chest height, feet shoulder-width. Sit into a full squat keeping elbows inside knees. For D-men, this builds the quad and hip strength needed for a low defensive stance.
Strength
Romanian Deadlift (RDL)
Hold dumbbells in front of thighs, hinge at hips with soft knees until you feel a hamstring stretch. Drive hips forward to stand. Builds the posterior chain — the power source behind every backward stride.
Single-Leg Jumps
Single-Leg Hop & Stick
Jump off one foot and land on the same foot. Freeze for 1–2 seconds before the next rep. The "stick" is the most important part. Foundation of all single-leg jump work in this program.
Single-Leg Jumps
Single-Leg Broad Jump
Take off from one foot, drive the knee up and reach forward, land on the same foot with a soft bent knee. Hold the landing 1–2 seconds. Tracked at Weeks 4, 8, and 12 to measure single-leg horizontal power growth.
Single-Leg Jumps
Single-Leg Vertical Jump
Drive off one foot straight up, swing the free knee upward for height, land back on the same foot with control. Touch a wall target to track height each week. Mirrors the push-off mechanics of a backward C-cut stride.
Single-Leg Jumps
Single-Leg Lateral Bound
Push off one foot sideways, land on the opposite foot and hold. For consecutive reps, immediately bound back. For D-men this directly mimics the lateral push-off in a defensive crossover — one of the most position-specific power movements in the program.
Single-Leg Jumps
Single-Leg Depth Landing
Step off a low platform (6–8") and land on ONE foot — soft knee, quiet landing, hold 2 seconds. Teaches the ankle, knee, and hip to absorb impact on a single leg — critical for the hard pivot plant move that every D-man makes multiple times per shift.

Fueling PerformanceNUTRITION BASICS
🍽 Fueling a Growing Hockey Player
  • Pre-training (60–90 min before): Carbs + small protein — oatmeal, toast with peanut butter, banana + yogurt
  • Post-training (within 30 min): Protein + carbs — chocolate milk, turkey sandwich, eggs + toast, Greek yogurt + fruit
  • Daily protein target: ~0.7g per pound of bodyweight (~52g/day). Chicken, eggs, dairy, beans, fish
  • Hydration: 60–80 oz water daily minimum. Add more on training days. No energy drinks.
  • Sleep: 9–10 hours every night. This is where growth hormone releases and muscles rebuild.
